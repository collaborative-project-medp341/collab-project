CLIMATE CHANGE - THE BASICS

According to NASA, climate change is “a long-term change in the average weather patterns that have come to define Earth’s local, regional and global climates.” During and after the Industrial Revolution, there has been a surge of climate change drive by human activities. The main activity is the burning of fossil fuels, which in turn allows the “greenhouse effect” to occur. The “greenhouse effect” traps heat within the Earth’s atmosphere causing the global temperature increase. Due to this change a number of effects are taking place, such as ocean and land temperature increases, ice melting, and variability is extreme weather (think hurricanes, droughts, floods, wildfires). Global Temperature

The Earth global temperature has risen about 1.63˚F (~0.9˚C). While this may not seem like a lot, this temperature continues to rise and would be detrimental to life as we know it. For example, according to a study published by the European Geoscience Union, a jump of 1.5˚C to 2.0˚C would allow heatwaves to last a third longer, coral reef survival to vanish, water deficit to double, and ecosystems to dismantle.

Carbon Dioxide Levels

Carbon dioxide is a greenhouse gas that takes a long time to leave the atmosphere. The IPCC Fifth Assessment Report states that 40% of the gas will remain in the atmosphere for 100 years, meaning that the CO2 that factories and cars release today are setting up the climate of our grandchildren. Moreover, CO2 levels are the highest they have been in centuries. Up until the 1950s, CO2 levels did not go over 300ppm, but today this number is ~410ppm.
